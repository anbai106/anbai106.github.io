Biography

I’m a postdoctoral researcher at University of Pennsylvania and supervised by Christos Davatzikos.

My research interests lie on the following aspects. First, my work focuses on developing and applying multimodal medical image analysis techniques (e.g., anatomical MRI and diffusion MRI) for biomarker identification and tracking for neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer's disease and frontotemporal lober degeneration disease. Secondly, I am also interested in developing machine learning (e.g., SVM and deep learning algorithms) decision system for disease diagnosis and prognosis. More generally, my work aims to advance the scientific reproducibility, transparency and objectivity in the domain of neuroscience and forward to the clinical translation.

For software developpment, I have contributed the developpment of the Clinica software platform, which is an open-source multimodal brain image analysis software. Besides, we have proposed our open-source framework for automatic diagnosis and prognosis of Alzheimer's disease using machine learning techniques (AD-ML).
